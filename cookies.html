Cookie Policy

Last Updated: February 24, 2026

What Are Cookies?

Cookies are small text files that are stored on your device when you visit a website. They help the website remember information about your visit, which can make it easier to visit the site again and make the site more useful to you.

We use cookies and similar tracking technologies to track activity on our Site and hold certain information. Cookies are used for various purposes including essential functionality, analytics, and advertising.

How We Use Cookies

We use cookies for the following purposes:

1. Essential Cookies (Strictly Necessary)

These cookies are necessary for the website to function and cannot be switched off. They are usually only set in response to actions made by you such as setting your privacy preferences or using calculator tools.

  • consent_preferences: Stores your cookie consent choices
  • session_data: Maintains your calculator session
  • theme_preference: Remembers your light/dark mode choice

2. Analytics Cookies (Performance)

These cookies allow us to count visits and traffic sources so we can measure and improve the performance of our site. They help us know which pages are the most and least popular and see how visitors move around the site.

  • Google Analytics (_ga, _gid, _gat): Anonymous traffic analysis
  • Purpose: Understand how visitors use our calculators, identify popular tools, detect technical issues
  • Data: Page views, session duration, bounce rates, device information

3. Advertising Cookies (Marketing)

These cookies may be set through our site by our advertising partners, including Google AdSense. They may be used to build a profile of your interests and show you relevant advertisements on other sites.

  • Google AdSense Cookies: Various cookies for ad personalization and measurement
  • Purpose: Display relevant ads, measure ad effectiveness, prevent fraud
  • Data: Interests, browsing behavior, ad interactions

Note: Advertising cookies support our ability to provide free calculator tools. You can control these cookies through the consent banner on your first visit.

Controlling Google AdSense Cookies

You can control how Google uses cookies for advertising:

  • Google Ad Settings - Control personalized ads
  • Google Analytics Opt-out - Disable Google Analytics
  • Use our cookie consent banner on first visit
  • Browser settings to block third-party cookies

Your Cookie Choices

You have several options to control cookies:

1. Cookie Consent Banner

On your first visit to our Site, you will see a cookie consent banner that allows you to:

  • Accept all cookies
  • Reject non-essential cookies
  • Customize your preferences by category
  • Learn more about each cookie type

2. Browser Settings

Most web browsers allow you to control cookies through their settings. You can:

  • Block all cookies (may break site functionality)
  • Block third-party cookies
  • Delete existing cookies
  • Set preferences for specific websites

Here are links to cookie settings for popular browsers:

  • Google Chrome
  • Mozilla Firefox
  • Apple Safari
  • Microsoft Edge

3. Do Not Track

Some browsers have a "Do Not Track" feature that tells websites you don't want to be tracked. While we respect this signal, not all tracking can be disabled while maintaining site functionality.
