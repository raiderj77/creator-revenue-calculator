How the Twitch revenue estimate works

The calculator adds four modeled revenue streams: subscriptions, Bits used to Cheer, advertising, and a sponsorship scenario. It uses a 50% subscription-share baseline, a blended Tier 1/Tier 2/Tier 3 subscriber mix, $0.01 for each Bit used to Cheer in channel chat, and an estimated ad CPM based on the selected category. Sponsorship is a planning scenario, not revenue Twitch promises or pays.

Core formulas

  • Subscriptions: estimated subscribers in each tier × the baseline creator share for that tier.
  • Bits: Bits used to Cheer × $0.01. Bits used inside extensions can be treated differently.
  • Ads: estimated impressions ÷ 1,000 × modeled CPM × 55% creator share.
  • Sponsorship: a broad scenario based on average concurrent viewers and category. It is not included because a brand is guaranteed to book.

Worked example

Consider a Just Chatting channel with 100 paid subscribers, 5,000 Bits used to Cheer, 100 average viewers, and 20 streamed hours per week. The model estimates about $290 from the blended subscription mix and $50 from Bits. With three modeled ad impressions per viewer-hour, 80 monthly hours, a $5.50 CPM, and a 55% share, ads contribute about $73. The sponsorship scenario adds $240, producing roughly $653 for the month. Without a booked sponsor, the same scenario is about $413.

How much does a streamer make per subscriber?

The calculator uses a baseline rather than claiming every creator receives the same amount. Local subscription pricing, taxes, refunds, Prime subscriptions, and the creator's agreement affect net revenue. Twitch's Plus Program offers qualifying Affiliates and Partners enhanced 60/40 or 70/30 net subscription shares for the applicable period.

How much are Bits worth?

Twitch Creator Camp states that eligible Partners and Affiliates receive $0.01 for every Bit used to Cheer directly in their channel. One hundred Bits therefore represents $1 to the creator in that use case. The viewer's purchase price and extension revenue rules are different from the channel-chat credit.

How many viewers do you need?

Eligibility and sustainable income are separate questions. A channel can qualify for monetization without earning a full-time income. Consistent concurrent viewers, returning subscribers, stream hours, geography, ad fill, and revenue outside Twitch all matter. Use multiple scenarios and compare the result with actual dashboard statements.

Limitations

This calculator does not model local subscription pricing, Prime subscription differences, refunds, taxes, ad fill, regional CPM, Plus Program qualification, extension Bits, donations, merchandise, agency fees, or contract-specific terms. It also cannot predict whether a sponsor will make an offer. Use the sponsorship rate calculator to model a specific deliverable and verify platform income against the Twitch dashboard.
