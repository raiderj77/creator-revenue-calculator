Compliance Tips for Finance YouTubers

1. Always Include Disclaimers

State clearly that your content is for educational purposes only and does not constitute financial advice. Include disclaimers in video descriptions and on-screen.

2. Disclose Affiliates & Sponsors

FTC guidelines require clear disclosure of sponsored content, affiliate links, and any financial relationships with companies you mention.

3. Avoid Specific Investment Advice

Providing specific buy/sell recommendations may require licensing (Series 65 or RIA). Stick to educational content and general principles.

4. Disclose Conflicts of Interest

If you hold positions in stocks or assets you discuss, disclose this to your audience. Transparency builds trust and protects you legally.

5. Keep Records

Document your content creation process, sponsorship agreements, and affiliate relationships. Good records protect you if questions arise.

6. Stay Current on Regulations

Financial regulations change frequently. Follow SEC, FTC, and platform-specific guidelines. Consider consulting a securities attorney as your channel grows.
