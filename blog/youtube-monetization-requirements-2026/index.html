YouTube Monetization Requirements 2026 — Complete Guide

Published March 28, 2026 · 7 min read

To join the YouTube Partner Program (YPP) in 2026, you need 1,000 subscribers and either 4,000 valid public watch hours in the past 12 months OR 10 million Shorts views in the past 90 days. After meeting both thresholds, YouTube manually reviews your channel — approval typically takes 1–4 weeks. You must also have an AdSense account and comply with all YouTube monetization policies.

The Two Paths to YouTube Monetization

YouTube offers two routes to the Partner Program in 2026. You only need to meet one, not both:

Path 1 — Long-form video: 1,000 subscribers + 4,000 valid public watch hours in the past 12 rolling months.

Path 2 — YouTube Shorts: 1,000 subscribers + 10 million valid public Shorts views in the past 90 days.

Most traditional creators use Path 1. Shorts-focused channels can reach monetization faster with viral content, but Shorts RPM is significantly lower than long-form video RPM. What YouTube Reviews After You Apply

Hitting the thresholds does not guarantee approval. YouTube manually reviews your channel against its monetization policies before granting access. The review covers:

  • Content policy compliance — no spam, no misleading content, no hate speech
  • Advertiser-friendly content — your videos must be suitable for ads
  • Copyright compliance — no unresolved copyright strikes
  • Community Guidelines strikes — your account must be in good standing
  • Authentic activity — YouTube checks for artificial inflation of subscribers or views

Most channels are approved within 1–4 weeks. Channels with content in gray areas (strong language, controversial topics, or graphic content) may take longer or receive limited monetization rather than full YPP status.

What You Get After YPP Approval

YouTube Partner Program membership unlocks multiple monetization features. Ad revenue from in-stream ads is the most discussed, but the full list is broader:

  • Ad revenue — in-stream ads, display ads, overlay ads. Revenue depends on CPM by niche.
  • Channel memberships — monthly recurring payments from subscribers ($0.99–$499/month tiers)
  • Super Thanks / Super Chat / Super Stickers — one-time payments from viewers
  • YouTube Shopping — product shelf integration if you have a connected store
  • YouTube Premium revenue — share of Premium subscription fees from your viewers

The YPP Thresholds Are Only the Beginning

Reaching YPP is the entry point, not the destination. A channel with exactly 1,000 subscribers and 4,000 watch hours earning $2 RPM on 10,000 monthly views generates roughly $20/month from ads. That is motivating proof that monetization works, but it is not a living income.

Most successful creators treat ad revenue as one of four to six income streams. Sponsorships typically pay 3–10x more per video than the equivalent ad revenue. A mid-size channel in a high-CPM niche can earn $1,000–$5,000 per sponsored integration once they pass 50,000 subscribers. Channel memberships and digital products can match or exceed ad revenue at the same subscriber level.

Frequently Asked Questions

What are the YouTube monetization requirements in 2026?

1,000 subscribers plus either 4,000 valid public watch hours in the past 12 months (long-form path) or 10 million Shorts views in the past 90 days (Shorts path). Both paths also require an active AdSense account and full compliance with YouTube monetization policies.

Anywhere from 2 months to 4+ years depending on upload frequency and average views. A channel posting 2 videos per week with 500 average views on 10-minute videos accumulates about 83 watch hours per week and reaches 4,000 hours in roughly 11 months.
