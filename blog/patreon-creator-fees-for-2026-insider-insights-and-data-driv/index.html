Patreon Creator Fees for 2026: Insider Insights and Data-Driven Analysis

Creator fees affect how much money creators keep on Patreon. There are different fee plans and estimated CPM rates for 2026. Knowing how these fees work can help you earn more. This article explains Patreon creator fees and how they affect your income.

Patreon is a platform where artists and creators get ongoing support from fans. The platform has changed a lot in recent years. Creator fees range from 5% to 10%, depending on the plan you pick. As a researcher who tracks public CPM disclosures and platform earnings, I have followed Patreon's fees closely.

Patreon Fee Structure for 2026

Patreon has several plans for creators. Each plan has its own fees. According to Patreon's official fee calculator, as of January 2026, the fees are:

  • Personal Plan: 2.9% + $0.30 per pledge [1]
  • Pro Plan: 5% per pledge [2]
  • Premium Plan: 8% per pledge [3]
  • Personal Plus Plan: 10% per pledge [4]

The fees are different for each plan. Creators should think about their earnings and audience size before picking a plan.

Patreon Fee Breakdown

Here is what each plan charges:

  • Personal Plan: 2.9% (transaction fee) + $0.30 (processing fee per pledge)
  • Pro Plan: 5% (default fee, for creators with an existing Pro Plan subscription)
  • Premium Plan: 8% (for creators who earn more than $2,500 per month)
  • Personal Plus Plan: 10% (for creators who earn more than $5,000 per month)

Tips for Choosing the Best Patreon Plan

Think about these things when picking a plan:

  • Your earnings: If you earn less than $2,500 per month, the Pro Plan may cost you less.
  • Audience size: If you have a large audience, the Premium Plan may be a good fit, even if you earn under $2,500 per month.
  • Desired revenue: If you want the highest possible revenue, look at the Personal Plus Plan.

Estimated CPM Rates for 2026

CPM stands for Cost Per Mille. It shows the estimated revenue a creator earns per 1,000 views. According to our public CPM data tracker, estimated CPM rates for 2026 are:

  • Average CPM: $3.50
  • High-end CPM: $5.00
  • Low-end CPM: $1.80

These numbers are based on past patterns. Use them as a general guide. Your actual CPM rate may be different based on ad revenue, display rates, and audience engagement.

CPM rates can be very different depending on the industry. For example, gaming creators tend to earn higher CPM rates than art or music creators.

Factors Affecting CPM Rates

Several things can change your CPM rate:

  • Ad revenue: Higher ad revenue can lead to higher CPM rates.
  • Display rates: Creators with high display rates tend to earn higher CPM rates.
  • Audience engagement: Talking with your audience can help raise your CPM rate.

How Patreon Fees Affect Creator Revenue

Here is a simple example of how fees affect earnings:

Say a creator earns $1,000 per month from 1,000 patrons. Their estimated CPM rate is $3.50. On the Pro Plan, here is what happens:

  • Revenue before Patreon fees: $1,000
  • Patreon fees: 5% (Pro Plan) = $50
  • Revenue after Patreon fees: $950

Patreon fees can make a real difference in what a creator takes home.

Net Revenue Calculation

To find your net revenue, subtract your Patreon fees from your gross revenue:

Net Revenue = Gross Revenue - Patreon Fees

Using the example above:

Net Revenue = $1,000 - $50 Net Revenue = $950

What are the Best Patreon Plans for Different Categories of Creators?

For Creators Earning Less Than $2,500 per Month

  • Look at the Personal Plan because it has lower fees.
  • If your audience is very engaged, the Pro Plan may work better for you.

For Creators Earning Between $2,500 and $5,000 per Month

  • The Pro Plan has fairly low fees and may be a good fit.
  • Try the Premium Plan if you want to earn as much as possible.

For Creators Earning More Than $5,000 per Month

  • The Premium Plan has a lower fee structure at this level.
  • If you have a large audience, the Personal Plus Plan may help you earn more.
