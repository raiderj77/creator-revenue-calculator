How Much Does Pinterest Pay Creators in 2026

Updated March 29, 2026 • Creator Revenue Calculator

Pinterest does not pay creators per view in 2026. The Creator Rewards program was discontinued. Creators earn money through affiliate links (commissions on purchases), brand partnerships ($100–$5,000 per Pin), and driving traffic to monetized blogs. Top Pinterest creators in focused niches earn $5,000–$50,000/month — but through sales commissions, not view counts.

Why Pinterest Does Not Pay Per View

Pinterest operates on a fundamentally different business model than YouTube, TikTok, or Facebook. Those platforms serve video ads against creator content and share the ad revenue. Pinterest is primarily a search and discovery platform — users come to find products, recipes, home decor ideas, and DIY projects. Pinterest's revenue model is built around shopping and product ads, not video ads against creator content.

The Pinterest Creator Rewards program, which did offer some direct creator payments, was discontinued in 2023. Pinterest has not replaced it with a per-view monetization system. Instead, the platform is leaning into its strength as a commerce platform — helping creators connect their audiences with purchasable products through affiliate links and brand partnerships.

This is not necessarily a disadvantage. Pinterest's user base has high purchase intent. People save Pins because they are planning to buy, make, or do something. A well-placed affiliate link on Pinterest can convert at rates significantly higher than the same link on a platform where users are primarily watching entertainment content.

How Pinterest Creators Actually Make Money in 2026

1. Affiliate Marketing (The Primary Income Source)

Affiliate marketing is the most scalable income stream for Pinterest creators. Pinterest allows direct affiliate links in Pins — you pin an image of a product, include your affiliate link, and earn a commission when someone clicks through and buys. No minimum follower count is required to start. The disclosure requirement is to tag sponsored or affiliate content with Pinterest's built-in "Paid Partnership" tool or clearly label the Pin.

Commission rates vary significantly by affiliate program. Amazon Associates pays 1% to 10% depending on product category. Specialty retailers and direct brand programs often pay 10% to 30%. Digital products (courses, software subscriptions, templates) frequently offer 30% to 50% recurring commissions. A creator pinning in the home decor niche with 50,000 monthly engaged viewers and a 2% click-through rate sending 1,000 people to an affiliate offer with a 5% purchase conversion at $50 average order value earns approximately $250 per month from that single affiliate relationship. Multiply that across 10 to 20 affiliate relationships, and the math compounds quickly.

2. Brand Partnerships and Sponsored Pins

Brand partnerships pay creators to create content featuring a specific product. These range from a single sponsored Pin to ongoing brand ambassador arrangements. Pinterest's "Paid Partnership" tool lets brands officially tag creator content, making the relationship transparent and allowing brands to amplify sponsored Pins as ads.

Rates depend on follower count, niche, and engagement quality:

  • 5,000–20,000 followers: $100–$500 per sponsored Pin
  • 20,000–100,000 followers: $500–$2,000 per sponsored Pin
  • 100,000+ followers: $2,000–$5,000+ per sponsored Pin
  • Board takeovers: $500–$3,000 per month for brands taking over a creator's board

Niche matters as much as follower count. A finance or home improvement creator with 10,000 highly engaged followers can command rates comparable to a lifestyle creator with 50,000 followers, because the audience's purchase intent and average order value are higher.

3. Blog Traffic Monetization

Pinterest is one of the strongest free traffic sources for bloggers. Creators who build a Pinterest presence in a content niche (recipes, home decor, DIY crafts, personal finance) and link Pins to their blog posts can drive substantial organic traffic that monetizes through display ads (AdSense, Mediavine, AdThrive) and affiliate links on the blog itself.

Display ad RPMs for blog content vary by niche. Mediavine's minimum traffic threshold is 50,000 sessions per month, and accepted publishers in home/lifestyle niches typically earn $15–$35 RPM. A blogger driving 100,000 monthly sessions from Pinterest at $25 RPM earns $2,500/month in display ad revenue alone — from traffic that Pinterest provided for free.

4. Pinterest Inclusion Fund (Grant-Based)

The Pinterest Inclusion Fund replaced the discontinued Creator Rewards program. It provides cash grants up to $25,000, ad credits, and creator mentorship to underrepresented creators using inclusive content features. This is not ongoing per-view income — it is a one-time or periodic grant. Participants have reported an average 60% follower increase and nearly 3x higher Idea Pin impressions after participation, which has a compounding effect on affiliate and brand deal income.

Best Affiliate Programs for Pinterest Creators

Choosing affiliate programs that match Pinterest's high-purchase-intent user base is critical. The best-performing affiliate categories on Pinterest align with the platform's strongest content niches:

Home Decor and DIY

Amazon Associates covers virtually any home product. Wayfair's affiliate program pays 5%–7% and converts well because users can immediately shop what they discovered on Pinterest. Home Depot and Lowe's both have affiliate programs through impact.com and CJ Affiliate for DIY content.

Fashion and Beauty

LTK (formerly rewardStyle) is the dominant fashion affiliate network and is natively integrated with Pinterest. ShopStyle Collective and Skimlinks aggregate multiple fashion brands. Commission rates range from 5% to 20% on fashion items. Beauty and skincare (Sephora, ULTA, direct brand programs) typically pay 5%–15%.

Crafts and Fiber Arts

Annie's craft subscription boxes pay commissions via ShareASale. Crafts supply retailers including JOANN, Hobby Lobby (through CJ Affiliate), and specialty yarn stores all run affiliate programs. These convert extremely well on Pinterest because the platform's craft community is large and project-focused.

Creator Tools and Digital Products

Software and creator tools pay the highest commissions (30%–50% recurring) and convert well to Pinterest's creator audience. Kit (formerly ConvertKit) pays 30% recurring commission for the life of the referred customer — one creator referral who stays on Kit for two years generates the equivalent of $172 in commission at the $9/month plan, compounding as they upgrade. For creators who teach others about building online income, email marketing and creator tool affiliates are among the highest-value programs available on any platform.

How to Grow Pinterest Income: The Strategy That Works

Pinterest income compounds differently than follower-based platforms. The algorithm distributes Pins based on relevance and Pin quality, not follower count. A well-optimized Pin in a niche with high search volume can drive traffic for 18–24 months. This means Pinterest income from a well-built account grows over time without proportionally more work.

Focus on search-first content

Pinterest is a visual search engine. Every Pin needs a keyword-optimized title and description for the Pinterest search algorithm to surface it. The same content strategy that works for Google SEO — target specific search queries, answer them directly, use the exact words people search for — works on Pinterest. Generic inspirational content that performs on Instagram typically underperforms on Pinterest, where users are searching for specific things.

Build around commercial-intent niches

The most profitable Pinterest niches are those where users are actively planning purchases: home renovation, wedding planning, fashion and outfit ideas, meal planning and recipes, DIY and craft projects, personal finance and budgeting. Content in these niches connects naturally to products, which makes affiliate links feel helpful rather than promotional.

Treat Pinterest as a traffic source, not a destination

The creators earning $10,000–$50,000/month on Pinterest are not earning that from Pinterest itself — they are earning it from what Pinterest traffic does when it reaches their blog, shop, or product page. Building a monetized destination (a blog with display ads and affiliate content, or an email list) and using Pinterest to drive traffic to it is the proven path to significant Pinterest income.

Pinterest Monetization: Common Questions

How long does it take to make money on Pinterest?

Most creators see their first affiliate commission within 1–3 months if they are actively pinning in a commercial niche. Significant income ($500+/month) typically takes 6–18 months of consistent content creation. Pinterest's compounding traffic model means income often accelerates in the second year as older Pins continue driving traffic and new Pins add to the base. Accounts that abandon after 3 months and return a year later often find their existing Pins are still driving traffic.

Do you need a lot of followers to make money on Pinterest?

No. Pinterest's algorithm is relevance-based, not follower-based. A new account can have a Pin seen by tens of thousands of people if it matches a popular search query well. Follower count matters more for brand partnerships than for affiliate income. Many affiliate marketers on Pinterest never prioritize growing followers — they optimize for Pin reach and click-through rate to their affiliate offers instead.

Is Pinterest better than Instagram for affiliate marketing?

For most niches, yes. Pinterest allows direct affiliate links in Pins without needing a link-in-bio workaround. Pinterest content has a much longer shelf life (months to years vs. days on Instagram). Pinterest users have higher purchase intent. The main advantage Instagram has is for fashion and beauty creators who are focused on personal brand sponsorships rather than affiliate commissions, where Instagram's influencer culture and follower-based rates favor large accounts.

What is the most profitable niche on Pinterest?

Home decor, personal finance, and wedding planning consistently rank as Pinterest's most profitable affiliate niches because they combine high purchase intent, high average order values, and strong content evergreen potential. Food and recipes drive massive traffic but lower affiliate commissions (Amazon kitchen products convert better than CPM ads for small blogs). DIY and crafts convert extremely well for craft supply affiliates, particularly through ShareASale programs like Annie's.

The Bottom Line on Pinterest Creator Income

Pinterest does not pay per view. It pays through commerce — affiliate commissions, brand deals, and the traffic it sends to your monetized content. The creators earning $5,000–$50,000/month on Pinterest have built focused, search-optimized accounts in niches with real purchase intent, connected their Pins to affiliate offers and monetized blogs, and treated Pinterest as a compounding traffic asset rather than a follower-growth game.

For comparison: YouTube pays $3–$22 per 1,000 views, TikTok pays $0.40–$1.00 per 1,000 views, and top YouTubers earn through a mix of AdSense and brand deals. Pinterest requires a different strategy — but for the right creator in the right niche, the income ceiling is comparable.
