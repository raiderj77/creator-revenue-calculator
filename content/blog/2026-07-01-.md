# Podcast Revenue Calculator: What Your Show Actually Earns (With Real Math)

> **Quick answer:** A podcast with 10,000 downloads per episode can realistically earn $200–$500 per episode from host-read sponsorships, $0–$80 from dynamic ad insertion, and $300–$1,500/month from listener support on Patreon, depending on niche, conversion rate, and audience trust. No single number applies universally. The math below shows you why.

---

## What Does a Podcast Calculator Actually Measure?

A podcast revenue calculator estimates income across three distinct buckets: sponsorships, dynamic ad insertion (DAI), and direct listener support. Each bucket has its own rate structure, and conflating them is where most income estimates go wrong.

The three buckets behave differently. Sponsorship rates are negotiated per deal. DAI rates are set by ad networks and fluctuate by quarter. Listener support depends entirely on your relationship with your audience, not your download count.

Most "podcast income calculators" online spit out a single number based on CPM alone. That ignores the fact that a 5,000-download true-crime show and a 5,000-download B2B SaaS show have completely different earning ceilings. Niche is a multiplier, not a footnote.

---

## What Is a Realistic CPM for Podcast Ads?

For host-read sponsorships, industry CPMs typically run $18–$50 per thousand downloads for a mid-roll placement, and $10–$25 for a pre-roll. [Spotify's 2023 advertising data](https://advertising.spotify.com/en-US/news-and-insights/podcast-advertising-effectiveness/) and [AdvertiseCast's published rate benchmarks](https://www.advertisecast.com/podcast-advertising-rates) both place the average host-read mid-roll around $25 CPM.

Here is what that looks like in practice:

- **10,000 downloads x $25 CPM mid-roll = $250 per episode**
- **10,000 downloads x $18 CPM pre-roll = $180 per episode**
- Two placements per episode: roughly **$430 gross per episode**

That is gross. Networks take a cut. If you go through a network like Spotify Audience Network or Acast, expect 30–40% to stay with the network, leaving you $258–$301 per episode on the example above.

Dynamic ad insertion through platforms like Buzzsprout Ads or Anchor (now Spotify for Podcasters) runs lower. [Spotify for Podcasters' own help documentation](https://support.spotify.com/us/podcasters/article/earning-money-from-your-podcast/) lists DAI payouts starting at around $2–$8 CPM depending on geography and targeting. At 10,000 downloads and a $5 CPM, that is $50 per episode. Not nothing, but not a living.

## How Many Downloads Do You Actually Need to Get Sponsors?

Most direct sponsorship deals require a minimum of 5,000–10,000 downloads per episode, though some niche B2B shows close deals at 1,000–2,000 downloads because the audience is high-value. [Edison Research's Infinite Dial 2024](https://www.edisonresearch.com/the-infinite-dial-2024/) places the median podcast audience size far below what most creators assume, with the majority of shows never crossing 1,000 downloads per episode.

That median matters for your calculator inputs. If you are at 800 downloads per episode, the honest math is:

- DAI revenue: ~$4–$6 per episode at a $5 CPM
- Direct sponsorship: unlikely unless your niche commands a premium
- Listener support: potentially $50–$300/month if you have a loyal core

Small audiences are not disqualifying. They just mean the Patreon or listener support bucket becomes your primary revenue channel, not ads.

---

## What Can You Realistically Earn From Patreon for a Podcast?

Patreon takes 8–12% of creator earnings depending on your plan tier, as stated in [Patreon's current pricing page](https://www.patreon.com/pricing). Payment processor fees add another 2.9% + $0.30 per transaction for payments over $3.

A rough conversion benchmark: 1–5% of your monthly listeners become paying patrons. [Patreon's own creator education resources](https://www.patreon.com/c/creatingonpatreon) suggest that engaged communities with consistent output convert at the higher end of that range.

Running the math for a show with 5,000 monthly unique listeners:

- 1% conversion = 50 patrons
- Average pledge of $7/month = $350 gross
- After Patreon's 8% cut and processor fees: roughly **$305/month net**

At 5% conversion with the same audience:

- 250 patrons x $7 = $1,750 gross
- Net: approximately **$1,520/month**

The difference between 1% and 5% conversion is almost entirely explained by how much the audience trusts the host and how clearly the value of membership is communicated. Download count is a secondary factor.

## How Do You Build a Podcast Revenue Calculator Yourself?

Use four inputs and keep the assumptions visible. Hidden assumptions are why most calculators mislead people.

**Input 1: Monthly downloads per episode**
Use your actual 30-day download figure from your hosting platform, not the all-time total.

**Input 2: CPM tier**
General entertainment: $15–$20. Personal finance, health, business: $25–$40. Highly targeted B2B or professional niches: $40–$80. These ranges align with [AdvertiseCast's public rate card](https://www.advertisecast.com/podcast-advertising-rates).

**Input 3: Patreon conversion rate**
Start with 1% as your conservative case. Use 3% as your realistic target. Do not plan around 5% until you have evidence your audience converts at that rate.

**Input 4: Number of ad placements per episode**
Two placements (one pre-roll, one mid-roll) is standard. Three is possible but risks listener drop-off, which affects long-term CPM viability.

Multiply downloads by CPM, divide by 1,000, multiply by placements, apply network cut if applicable. Add Patreon projection separately. That is your range, not your guarantee.

---

## What the Calculator Cannot Tell You

The calculator gives you a ceiling based on inputs. It cannot account for advertiser churn, seasonal CPM drops (Q1 is consistently the lowest CPM quarter across digital advertising, per [IAB's annual Digital Advertising Revenue Report](https://www.iab.com/insights/iab-internet-advertising-revenue-report/)), or the reality that most sponsorship deals are not evergreen.

A show earning $1,200/month from two sponsors loses half its revenue the moment one sponsor pauses. That is not a hypothetical. It is the normal operating condition for independent podcasts without a network contract.

Build the calculator. Run the numbers honestly. Then build a revenue mix that does not collapse if one bucket goes to zero.

## Frequently asked questions

### Сколько можно заработать на подкасте с 1000 слушателями?
С 1000 слушателями подкаст может приносить от $50 до $500 в месяц в зависимости от монетизации. Спонсорские интеграции по модели CPM обычно платят $18–$50 за тысячу загрузок, тогда как Patreon или Substack могут добавить стабильный доход от подписчиков. Реальный заработок сильно зависит от ниши, вовлечённости аудитории и количества рекламных мест в эпизоде.

### Какой CPM у подкастов по сравнению с YouTube?
CPM подкастов обычно выше, чем у YouTube, и составляет $18–$50 против $2–$10 на YouTube. Это объясняется высокой вовлечённостью аудитории подкастов и доверием к ведущему. Рекламодатели платят премию за хост-риды, поскольку слушатели воспринимают рекомендации более лично. Однако объём аудитории YouTube часто компенсирует разницу в CPM для крупных каналов.

### Когда подкаст начинает приносить реальный доход?
Большинство подкастов начинают зарабатывать заметные деньги при достижении 5000–10 000 загрузок в месяц. До этого порога спонсоры редко проявляют интерес, а доход от Patreon остаётся минимальным. Использование калькулятора доходов помогает реалистично оценить сроки и выбрать правильную стратегию монетизации с самого начала, не дожидаясь больших цифр.

### Что выгоднее для подкастера, Patreon или Substack?
Substack выгоднее, если вы готовы регулярно писать дополнительный контент, так как платная рассылка конвертирует лучше среди образованной аудитории. Patreon проще для чисто аудио-формата и предлагает гибкие уровни поддержки. Оба инструмента берут комиссию около 5–12%. Лучшая стратегия, протестировать оба варианта и использовать калькулятор, чтобы сравнить потенциальный доход при вашей аудитории.

### Как рассчитать доход от спонсорства подкаста?
Доход от спонсорства рассчитывается по формуле: (загрузки ÷ 1000) × CPM × количество рекламных мест. Например, 10 000 загрузок с двумя интеграциями по CPM $25 дадут $500 за эпизод. Важно учитывать, что спонсоры часто требуют минимум 3000–5000 загрузок на эпизод. Калькулятор на сайте автоматизирует этот расчёт и показывает реалистичный потенциал при разных сценариях роста.
